Worry Diary

Track anxious situations, your thoughts, emotions, physical sensations, and how you responded. Regular practice helps you notice patterns and build awareness of your coping strategies.

How to use this diary

Fill this in as soon as you can after a worrying or anxious episode, while the details are still fresh. Work through each section honestly — the more specific you are, the more useful the patterns become over time.

There are no right or wrong answers. The goal is observation, not judgement. You're building a picture of how your anxiety works so you can start to work with it, not against it.

"Worrying does not empty tomorrow of its troubles — it empties today of its strength."
