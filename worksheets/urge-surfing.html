The 15-Minute Rule & Urge Surfing

Two powerful tools for riding out the wave — delay the response, observe the urge with curiosity, and let it pass on its own without giving in.

How to use this worksheet

When an obsessive thought, compulsive urge, or overwhelming craving arises, the instinct is to act immediately to relieve the discomfort. This worksheet guides you through two techniques — the 15-Minute Rule and Urge Surfing — that together teach your brain that the urge will pass on its own, without you needing to give in.

Use it in the moment as an urge arises, or fill it in shortly afterwards while the experience is still fresh. Over time, your entries will build evidence that you can tolerate discomfort — and that every wave always breaks.

⏱ The 15-Minute Rule

When the urge strikes, commit to a 15-minute pause before acting. Use the time to label the thought, reattribute it to a brain signal rather than a truth, and refocus your attention on something else. After 15 minutes, reassess — the urge is often weaker. If not, wait another 15.

🌊 Urge Surfing

Instead of fighting or giving in to an urge, observe it with curiosity. Notice where you feel it in your body. Visualise it as a wave — building to a peak, then breaking. Stay with the sensation, breathe, and let the wave subside. Urges typically fade within 20–30 minutes if not fed.
