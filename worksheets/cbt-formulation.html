Formulation Sheet

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

A CBT Formulation Sheet is a structured tool that helps map out how your background, early experiences, and current stressors connect to the difficulties you are facing today. Working through this sheet — ideally with your therapist — builds a shared understanding of your problems and points towards a clear way forward.

🔗 Connect

Understand the links between your past experiences, early beliefs, and present-day difficulties

🧩 Understand

Make sense of why you developed certain patterns and how they continue to affect you today

🎯 Focus

Identify the maintaining factors keeping your difficulties in place — and where to intervene

🌿 Move Forward

Draw on your assets and strengths to chart a meaningful, realistic path towards change

How to Use This Sheet

Work through each section in order, from top to bottom. This formulation is not a fixed diagnosis — it is a working map that can be updated as your understanding deepens. Complete it honestly, and discuss it with your therapist to refine it over time.

1

Background Factors

Consider the biological (e.g. genetics, health), social (e.g. family environment, culture), and psychological (e.g. early trauma, significant relationships) factors and early life experiences that have shaped who you are and how you see the world.

2

Early Coping Style

Think about how you responded to your early experiences. What core beliefs did you develop about yourself, others, and the world? What assumptions did you form? How were these reflected in your behaviour, emotions, and physical responses?

3

Recent Stresses / Triggers

What recent events, situations, or changes have activated or worsened your difficulties? These might be life events, relationship changes, health issues, or work pressures that have brought old patterns to the surface.

4

Recent Maintaining Factors

What is keeping your difficulties going right now? Record the physical sensations, emotions, thoughts, and behaviours that form a self-reinforcing cycle. These are the key targets for change in CBT.

5

Assets

What personal strengths, skills, values, relationships, and resources do you have? Assets might include resilience, creativity, supportive relationships, problem-solving ability, or previous experience of overcoming difficulties.

6

Way Forward

Based on everything above, what specific steps, goals, or changes might help? This is where your formulation becomes a plan — grounded in your own understanding of how your difficulties developed and what might shift them.

💡 Remember: A formulation is a hypothesis, not a verdict. It is a shared attempt to make sense of your experiences — not a fixed label. It will evolve as you and your therapist learn more. Treat it with curiosity rather than judgement.
