The 333 Rule

A grounding technique to bring you back to the present moment when anxiety, panic, or overwhelm sets in.

How It Works

The 333 Rule works by redirecting your attention from racing thoughts and internal panic toward the present, physical environment. From a CBT perspective, it activates three key mechanisms:

Grounding

Shifts attention away from anxious thoughts toward the external world around you.

Brain Regulation

Activates the prefrontal cortex (rational brain) and calms the amygdala (the brain's fear centre).

Breaking the Cycle

Offers an immediate, actionable way to interrupt the spiral of anxious thoughts before they escalate.

When to Use the 333 Rule

  • During a panic attack or moment of high stress
  • When feeling overwhelmed, anxious, or unable to focus
  • When you feel "frozen" or disconnected from the present moment

First, Pause

Take one slow breath if you can.

You do not need to change your breathing. Just notice it.

Bring Attention to Your Body

Place your feet on the floor if possible. Notice the weight of your body being supported.

A Simple Reassuring Statement

Choose one statement that feels most helpful right now, or write your own below.
