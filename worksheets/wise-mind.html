Understanding the Three Minds

We all move between Rational Mind and Emotional Mind depending on the situation. Neither is bad — both are essential. The goal is to develop the capacity to access Wise Mind: where thoughts and feelings integrate and we experience a deeper, centred knowing.

🧠 Rational Mind

Thinking, logical, evidence-based. Focused on facts, planning, organising and problem-solving. These skills are primarily housed in the frontal lobes of the brain.

✨ Wise Mind

Integration of the rational and emotional. Intuition. A quiet sense of peace, knowing, and being centred — when thoughts and feelings come together as one.

❤️ Emotional Mind

Strong emotional states arising from the Amygdala. Feelings rooted in early experience that can both enrich life and influence or distort thinking.

How to use this worksheet

Work through the four exercises below. The goal is not to eliminate either Rational or Emotional Mind — both are valuable — but to deepen your self-awareness and increase your access to Wise Mind: the integrated, grounded state where your best decisions are made.

1
The Benefits of Your Rational Mind
🧠 Rational Mind

Thinking rationally is an enormous asset. What does your Rational Mind help you with in your life? When do you rely on it most — and what would be harder without it?

↓
2
The Benefits of Your Emotional Mind
❤️ Emotional Mind

Emotional Mind is not the enemy of good thinking — it is also essential. It drives creativity, connection, love, passion, and moral courage. What does your Emotional Mind give you that logic alone cannot?

↓
3
A Time When You Were in Wise Mind
✨ Wise Mind

Wise Mind is the state where thoughts and feelings come together — where you know and feel something to be true at the same time, from your core. It is often quiet, calm, and certain without being cold or detached. Recall and describe a specific moment when you experienced this.

What Wise Mind can feel like A moment of profound clarity despite ongoing difficulty. Knowing you had to end a relationship or leave a job — not impulsively, but with deep, settled certainty. A moment of genuine forgiveness that felt complete. A creative decision that felt undeniably right. A time when your gut and your head finally agreed and you felt at peace with the knowing.
↓
4
Your Balance of the Three Minds

Consider how you tend to operate. Most of us lean more heavily on one type of mind — and most of us have blind spots. Honest reflection here is the beginning of greater balance.

Most of Which type of mind do you use most?
Too much of Which do you sometimes have too much of — and how does that cause problems?
Develop Which do you most need to develop — and what might that look like in practice?
✨ Remember: Wise Mind is not a permanent state — it is something to move towards. The more you practise noticing when you are purely in Rational or Emotional Mind, the more you will find your way to the integration that lies between them.
