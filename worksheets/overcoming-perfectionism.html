How to use this worksheet

Work through each exercise in order. The goal is not to eliminate high standards — it is to loosen the grip of perfectionism so that it no longer controls how you feel about yourself. Take your time, be honest, and treat this as an experiment rather than something else to get right.

1
Weigh Up Your Perfectionism

The first step is to honestly examine whether perfectionism is actually helping you. List the advantages and disadvantages of your own perfectionism — be as specific and honest as you can.

Three things to remember about imperfection

🧠 We learn from mistakes — we will not grow unless we allow ourselves to get things wrong.

🤝 People connect with vulnerability — most people feel more comfortable with someone who is "imperfect" and human.

🚫 Fear of failure leads to paralysis — if we are too afraid to make mistakes, we often stop trying altogether.

↓
2
Deliberately Limit Time and Lower Standards

Identify a task you would normally approach in a perfectionist way and set a strict time limit so that you genuinely cannot do it perfectly. The aim is to attempt the task at around 80% of your usual standard — then notice what actually happens.

Examples Instead of "I will write this report properly" — try "I have 20 minutes to write a draft and then I stop." Instead of "I will tidy the whole house" — try "I have 30 minutes, so that is 5 minutes per room."
↓
3
Disclose a Weakness

Make a deliberate attempt to show vulnerability to someone. If you feel nervous or out of your depth in a situation, say so. Dare to be average — and notice how the other person actually responds.

The challenge Perfectionists often keep emotions under very tight control. This exercise is a small, deliberate step in the other direction. You do not need to share anything major — even a small admission like "I found that really difficult" or "I wasn't sure what I was doing there" counts.
↓
4
Deliberately Savour the Moment

Choose an activity and consciously focus on the experience of doing it rather than the end result. Slow down. Notice what you are actually feeling and sensing in the moment, rather than rushing to reach the goal.

Why this matters Perfectionists tend to live in the future — always focused on the outcome and whether it will measure up. This exercise trains attention back onto the present, where enjoyment actually lives.
🌿 Remember: Perfectionism is not really about high standards — it is about fear. The antidote is not lowering your ambitions, but loosening the belief that your worth depends on the outcome. Good enough, done with care, is something to be genuinely proud of.
