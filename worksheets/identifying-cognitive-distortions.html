🧠 Identifying Cognitive Distortions

Cognitive distortions are mental shortcuts that exaggerate, misinterpret, or simplify reality in unhelpful ways. Use this worksheet to spot them in your thinking.

All-or-Nothing Thinking

Seeing things in extremes: success or failure, good or bad.

Catastrophising

Expecting the worst possible outcome.

Mind Reading

Assuming you know what others think.

Overgeneralisation

One event becomes a rule for everything.

Personalisation

Taking responsibility for things outside your control.

Emotional Reasoning

Believing something is true because it feels true.
