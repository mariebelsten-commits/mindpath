4-2-6 Breathing

During stress, the body switches on its "stress response" — fast breathing, a racing heart, tense muscles. Slow, steady breathing triggers the opposite "relaxation response," helping your breathing slow, your heart rate settle, and symptoms ease.

🌊 Stress vs Relaxation

The relaxation response is the body's natural off-switch for stress. Slow breathing is one of the quickest ways to activate it.

⏱️ The 4-2-6 Rhythm

Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 2, exhale for 6. The longer exhale is what signals your body to calm down.

🔁 Build the Habit

Practise twice a day for 10–20 breaths. Once it feels familiar, use it anytime anxiety arises.

🧘 Guided Breathing Circle

Find a safe, calm space and sit comfortably. Press start and breathe in time with the circle — it expands as you inhale, holds, then shrinks as you exhale. Breathe in and out through your nose.
