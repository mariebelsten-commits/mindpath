Be Clear About The Problem

Understanding your problems precisely is the essential first step in CBT. This worksheet helps you map what you want to change, how much it affects you, and the feelings and thoughts that come with it.

🎯

Get Specific

Vague problems are hard to solve. Name exactly what you want to start doing, stop doing, or do differently.

📊

Rate the Impact

Putting a number on how much each problem interferes with your life helps you see it more clearly and track progress.

🧠

Map Your Inner World

Seeing your feelings and thoughts laid out alongside each problem reveals the full picture of what's going on.

🚀

Set Priorities

Use the ratings to decide which problems are most important to tackle first in your CBT work.

How It Works

A step-by-step guide

Work through one problem per row. You don't need to fill in every row — focus on the problems that feel most relevant right now.

1

Identify what you want to change

In the first column, describe a specific behaviour you want to start, stop, or change. Be as concrete as possible — avoid vague descriptions like “feel better.”

2

Rate how much it interferes with your life

Give a percentage from 0 to 100 to show how much this problem gets in the way of the life you want to live. 0 = no interference at all, 100 = completely prevents you from living normally.

3

Name the feelings you don’t like

Write down the emotions associated with this problem — for example: anxious, ashamed, hopeless, angry, sad. Then rate how strongly you feel them (0–100%).

4

Identify the thoughts you don’t like

Write the unhelpful thoughts that come with this problem — for example: “I’m not good enough”, “Things will never improve.” Then rate how strongly you believe each one (0–100%).

5

Use this as your starting map

This worksheet gives you and your therapist — or your future self — a clear picture of where you are now. You can return to it later to see how things have changed.

💡 Tip: You don't need to fill in every row in one sitting. Work through as many problems as feel relevant today, and come back to add more as they become clearer.

🌿 Remember
