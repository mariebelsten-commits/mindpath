What is guilt?

Guilt is a complex emotion that tends to arise when we interpret one of our actions as having broken an internal moral code. Guilt focuses on what we have done to harm others and our desire to repair that hurt. A related — but different — emotion is shame, which involves the whole sense of self being inferior or bad, not just a specific behaviour.

People can experience too much or too little guilt. Those who feel chronic guilt often take too much responsibility for bad outcomes; others deflect entirely and blame those around them. The goal is to own your part — exactly your part — with honesty and proportion.

Appropriate Guilt and Moving On

We do not learn without making mistakes, and it is impossible to go through life without making blunders. It is right to feel remorse when you have acted hurtfully — but good people do wrong things without being bad people. Use these prompts to take responsibility, express your regret, and commit to change.

I wish I had…
💚 Remember: The goal is not to punish yourself indefinitely but to feel proportionate remorse, learn, repair where possible, and move forward. Carrying guilt beyond its useful purpose helps no one.
