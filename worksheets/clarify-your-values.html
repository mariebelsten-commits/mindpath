Your Values

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

Values are what we find meaningful in life. They are what you care about and consider to be important. Values are different for everybody, and they can change over time.

🧭 Direction

Values are like compass directions — they guide how you want to live, rather than destinations to reach

💡 Clarity

Understanding your values helps you make decisions that feel meaningful and aligned with who you want to be

⚖️ Awareness

Noticing gaps between your values and how you currently live can highlight areas where change would feel most rewarding

🌿 Growth

Living in accordance with your values — even in small ways — builds wellbeing, motivation, and self-respect

What Are Values?

Values are different from goals. Put crudely, goals can be 'achieved' whereas values are more like compass directions that we want to head in. For example, we might have the goal of getting our children to school on time, which sits within the value of 'being a good parent', or the goal of going for a jog while placing value upon exercise and physical health.

The domains below are valued by some people. Leaving aside any obstacles for the moment, think about what is important to you, and what you think makes for a meaningful life that you could value.

1

Describe Your Values

For each domain, write a brief description of what you value. For example: "to be a caring partner" (marriage), "to never stop learning" (education), or "to deepen my relationship with nature" (spirituality). There are no right or wrong answers.

2

Rate Importance

Rate how important each domain is to you from 0 (not important at all) to 10 (extremely important). Be honest — some domains may genuinely matter less to you than others, and that is completely valid.

3

Rate Success

Rate how successfully you have lived in accordance with this value in the past month, from 0 (not at all well) to 10 (very well). Where the gap between Importance and Success is largest, small actions can make the biggest difference.

💡 Reflection Prompt: After completing the worksheet, look for the domains where your Importance score is high but your Success score is low. These gaps often point to the most meaningful areas for change — and the smallest steps in those directions can have an outsized effect on your wellbeing.
