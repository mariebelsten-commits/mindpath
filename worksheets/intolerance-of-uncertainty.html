How to use this worksheet

Start by describing a belief you hold about uncertainty and rating how strongly you believe it. Identify a situation that triggers this belief and note your usual coping responses. Then design a small real-world experiment to test the belief — ideally dropping safety behaviours to make it a fair test. Afterwards, record what actually happened and re-rate your belief. Add as many experiment records as you need.

Life is uncertain. Nobody can ever be 100% sure of what will happen next. Some people are comfortable with this — but others find it deeply unpleasant. Psychologists call this intolerance of uncertainty, and it can drive significant anxiety and worry. Even a small amount of uncertainty can trigger a large reaction.

The good news is that our beliefs about uncertainty are not facts — they are learned assumptions that can be examined and updated. By exploring and testing these beliefs with real experiments, we can begin to build a more flexible, balanced relationship with the unknown.

🌿 Remember: The goal is not to eliminate uncertainty — that is impossible. It is to discover that you can tolerate it better than you currently believe. Each experiment, however small, builds real evidence for this. Approach each one with curiosity rather than pressure.
