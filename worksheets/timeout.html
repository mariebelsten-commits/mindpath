Time-Out Technique

A structured way to step away from a heated situation, calm down safely, and return ready to resolve things constructively — without saying or doing something you'll regret.

What is the Time-Out Technique?

When anger escalates, our ability to think clearly shuts down. The time-out technique gives you a structured way to physically remove yourself from a heated situation, allow adrenaline and cortisol levels to fall, and return to the conversation with a calmer, more rational mindset.

The key is that a time-out is not a punishment, and not abandonment — it is a coping tool. Used consistently, it prevents destructive behaviour, protects relationships, and builds trust that difficult conversations will be resolved, not avoided.

Key Steps

  • Recognise your cues early — physical and emotional signs that you're reaching your limit
  • Signal the time-out calmly — let the other person know you need a break to calm down, not to win
  • Set a clear time limit — agree a specific return time to build trust that you're coming back
  • Physically leave the space — removing yourself from the environment breaks the cycle of escalation
  • Calm down — don't dwell — use the time for walking, breathing, or music; do not ruminate on the argument
  • Return and resolve — re-engage when calmer to discuss the issue constructively
