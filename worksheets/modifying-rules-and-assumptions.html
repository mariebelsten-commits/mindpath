How It Works

A step-by-step guide

Work through each section in order. Take your time — honest reflection leads to the most useful results.

1

Identify the old rule

Write down the rule or assumption you want to work on. It often sounds like: "If I don't… then…" or "I must always…"

2

Explore its origins

Reflect on where this rule came from. What experiences, messages, or relationships helped form it?

3

Weigh up advantages & disadvantages

Consider how this rule has helped you — and how it has held you back or caused you difficulty.

4

Examine the evidence

Look for real examples that support or challenge the rule. Try to be as balanced and specific as possible.

5

Build a new rule & action plan

Write a more flexible, compassionate rule — then plan the small steps that will help you live by it.

💡 Tip: Rules and assumptions are not facts — they are learned patterns. Noticing them is the first and most important step towards changing them.

📝 Before you begin

Rules and assumptions are the "if–then" beliefs that govern how we act. For example: "If I show weakness, people will reject me" or "I must always put others first." They often developed for good reasons but can become too rigid over time.

Step 1 — Identify Your Old Rule or Assumption

Write the rule as clearly as you can. Try using "If… then…" or "I must / I should…" format.

Step 2 — Where Did This Rule Come From?

Step 7 — Behavioural Changes to Make

Step 8 — Possible Obstacles

What might get in the way of living by your new rule? Think about situations, feelings, or habits that could pull you back.

▼

Step 9 — How I Will Overcome These Obstacles

Plan how you will respond when you notice the old rule taking over. What will you say to yourself? Who or what can help?

🌿 Remember

Changing deeply held rules takes time and repeated practice. Every time you choose to act according to your new rule — even in a small way — you are rewiring old patterns. Be patient and compassionate with yourself throughout this process.
