Balanced Thinking

Balanced thinking helps you see situations more clearly and respond in ways that are grounded and realistic. Instead of automatically believing your first thought, look at all sides and form a more helpful, accurate perspective.

⚖️ Weigh the Evidence

Look at the facts for and against your thought — not just how it feels. Feelings are valid, but they aren't always facts.

🔍 Ask Fairer Questions

Guided questions help you step back, challenge unhelpful thinking, and find a kinder, more realistic view.

📉 Notice the Shift

Rate the intensity of your emotion before and after. Balanced thinking often softens the strength of difficult feelings.

4

Balanced Thinking Questions

Use these questions to help you find a fairer, more balanced view.

  • What might I tell a friend if they had this thought?
  • Am I confusing a possibility with a certainty?
  • What are other ways to see this situation?
  • What would I say if I were being kind to myself?
  • What is the bigger picture?
5

My Balanced Thought

Write a statement that feels calm, fair, and compassionate.

Example: "I made a mistake, but I can learn from it. One mistake doesn't mean I'm a failure."
