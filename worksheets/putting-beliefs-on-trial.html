The Courtroom

The Judge & Jury

The Judge's Role

The Judge presides over the trial, overseeing the entire process — impartial, rational, and committed to finding the truth.

The Judge's task is to ensure that all evidence — both for and against — is heard fairly before delivering a final, balanced verdict.

The Jury's Role

Members of the Jury hear the evidence to determine the facts and deliver a verdict — guilty or not guilty — based on that evidence.

They remain impartial, listen to evidence from both the prosecution and the defence, and follow the judge's legal directions.
