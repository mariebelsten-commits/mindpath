5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

A five-senses technique to anchor you in the present moment and ease anxiety, overwhelm, or dissociation.

What is this technique? The 5-4-3-2-1 exercise is a sensory-based grounding tool that interrupts anxious thought spirals by shifting your focus to your immediate physical environment. By deliberately engaging each of your five senses — sight, touch, hearing, smell, and taste — you anchor yourself in the present moment and reduce the intensity of distressing thoughts. It is especially useful during panic attacks, periods of intense stress, or when you feel detached or overwhelmed.

How to Practise

  1. Take a slow, deep breath — allow your body to begin to settle before you start.
  2. See 5 things: Look around and name five things you can see right now.
  3. Feel 4 things: Focus on textures or sensations on your skin or in your body.
  4. Hear 3 things: Notice sounds in your environment — both near and far.
  5. Smell 2 things: If nothing stands out immediately, think of a scent you enjoy.
  6. Taste 1 thing: This could be a lingering flavour, a sip of water, or a mint.
