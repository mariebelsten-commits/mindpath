My Common Thinking Traps

Thinking traps are quick shortcuts the mind uses to make sense of events. They can be helpful in danger, but in everyday life they often distort meaning, intensify emotion, and steer behaviour in unhelpful directions. Identifying your most common traps helps you respond with more balance.

My Early Warning Signs

What are the first clues that a thinking trap is starting? Early warning signs might be physical (tight chest, shallow breathing), emotional (sudden anxiety, irritability), or linguistic (noticing "always," "never," "everyone," "I can't" appearing in your thoughts).

What This Tells Me

Now that you have identified your most common thinking traps and early warning signs, take a moment to reflect on what this means for you.

🌿 Remember: Noticing a thinking trap does not mean your thoughts are wrong or that you are broken. It simply means your mind is doing what all minds do — taking shortcuts. The more you practise noticing these patterns, the more space you create between the thought and your response to it.
